The MVP of Afterhours focuses on delivering a calm, welcoming experience supported by a simple and reliable digital system. The goal is to validate the concept in real life while keeping development manageable for a single developer.

## MVP Goals
- Support the physical Afterhours space with minimal but useful software
- Enable memberships and access without complexity
- Avoid unnecessary automation or over-engineering
- Deliver a system that feels human-first

## Included in MVP ‚úÖ

### 1. User Registration & Profiles
- Basic user signup (name, phone/email)
- User category (student, working professional, guest)
- Simple profile view

### 2. Digital Membership Plans
- Time-based plans:
  - 2 weeks
  - 1 month
  - 3 months
- Each plan includes predefined benefits (drinks, meals, access)

### 3. Digital Card System üí≥
- Auto-generated digital card for each user
- Shows:
  - Active plan
  - Validity period
  - Remaining benefits
- Used for entry and ordering

- Create and manage membership plans
- Add or remove user benefits

### 5. Coupon & Offer System üéÅ
- Admin-created coupons
- Assigned to users or categories
- Redeemable during ordering

### 6. Basic Usage Tracking
- Track:
  - Entry visits
  - Benefit usage (tea/coffee/meals)
- Simple logs (no analytics yet)

## Not Included in MVP ‚ùå
(Will be part of future versions)

- AI recommendations
- Mobile app
- Automated payments
- Facial recognition / biometric entry
- Advanced analytics or dashboards
- Social feeds or chat features

## MVP Success Criteria
The MVP is successful if:
- Users can register and receive a digital card
- Memberships can be activated and recharged
- Admin can manage users and benefits easily
- People feel the system is simple and friendly
- The physical space experience is improved, not interrupted

This MVP is designed to be completed by a single developer within an academic semester and presented as a functional real-world system.
